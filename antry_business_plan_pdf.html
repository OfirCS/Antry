01 — Executive Summary

The way developers get hired is fundamentally broken. Resumes and LinkedIn profiles describe what someone has done in the past — but the best builders today are proving their value by shipping real AI agents and products in hours, not months. There is no dedicated platform for this generation of builders to be discovered, celebrated, and connected with opportunities.

Antry fills this gap with a three-layer platform: a showcase layer for builder portfolios, a community layer built around hackathons and sponsored events, and a studio layer that co-builds and validates the most promising ideas from within the community.

"We're building a community where developers prove their skills through projects and demos instead of resumes — and we help the best ones turn ideas into real products."

The business model is capital-efficient: we start with sponsorships and placement fees (no upfront investment needed), grow through community momentum, and layer in premium products and studio equity as we scale.

02 — The Problem

Hiring is broken for the AI-native builder

The current hiring process for technical talent was designed for a different era. It relies on credentials, job titles, and years of experience — metrics that have almost no correlation with the ability to build fast, iterate quickly, and ship working AI products.

For builders

Talented developers who build impressive AI agents and demos have no dedicated place to showcase their work. GitHub shows code. LinkedIn shows credentials. Neither shows what actually matters — working, live products.

For companies

Companies hiring AI builders are increasingly asking for demos, not CVs — but they have no efficient way to discover, evaluate, or recruit talent based on shipped work. The discovery layer is missing.

The "vibe coding" generation — developers who use AI tools to build faster than ever — is growing rapidly. This group needs its own platform, its own community, and its own way of proving value. Antry is that platform.

03 — The Solution

Three layers, one ecosystem

Antry is built in three layers, each one compounding on the last. You don't need all three to launch — you start with the showcase, grow the community, then activate the studio. Every layer creates value for the next.

Layer 01

Showcase — Builder Portfolios

Every builder gets a profile built around live demos, project walkthroughs, and shipped agents. Each project has its own shareable link with a rich preview image optimized for social media. Think Dribbble for builders — your work speaks, not your job title. This is the core product and where we start.

Layer 02

Community — Hackathons & Sponsored Events

Themed hackathons and build events sponsored by companies like Anthropic, AWS, and OpenAI. Builders compete, network, and gain visibility. Sponsors get direct access to the best technical talent in the world. Events are heavily documented on social media to drive organic growth. Each hackathon also seeds the showcase layer with fresh demo content.

Layer 03

Studio — Launch & Validate

Antry identifies high-potential builders and ideas from within the community. We run quick validation sprints — typically 2–4 weeks. If an idea shows traction, Antry co-builds alongside the founder and takes a small equity stake. This is a venture studio model powered entirely by community signal, with no cold sourcing required. Phase 3 only activates once the community is large enough to surface real signal.

06 — Go-to-Market Strategy

Community-led, social-first growth

Our go-to-market strategy is designed for maximum impact with minimum spend. We don't need paid advertising to reach our audience — they live on X/Twitter, Discord, and GitHub, and they respond to authentic community building.

Phase 1 — Seed (Month 1)

Launch the waitlist, build social proof

Deploy the landing page with a waitlist. Post on X, LinkedIn, relevant Discord servers (AI builders, indie hackers, developer communities). Target 500 signups before the platform launches. Use waitlist numbers as leverage to approach first sponsors.

Phase 2 — First Event (Month 2)

Invite-only hackathon with 50 builders

Run the first themed hackathon (AI agents theme recommended for maximum sponsor interest). Invite 50 curated builders from the waitlist and developer communities. Secure 1–2 sponsors ($5K–$15K). Document everything on social media. The demos produced become the seed content for the platform.

Phase 3 — Platform Launch (Month 3)

Open builder profiles, seed with hackathon content

Launch the full platform with hackathon demos already live. Use hackathon participants as early advocates who share their profiles. Begin outreach to companies for talent placement. Target 200 active builder profiles within 30 days of launch.

Phase 4 — Scale (Month 4–12)

Regular cadence, growing sponsor base

One hackathon every 6–8 weeks. Grow builder profiles to 500+ by Month 6, 2,000+ by Month 12. Expand sponsor roster to 5–10 active companies. Begin testing premium profiles. Launch studio pilot with 2–3 teams.

11 — Why Now

The timing has never been better

Three converging trends make 2026 the right moment to launch Antry:

AI agent explosion

The number of developers building AI agents has grown exponentially in the past 18 months. Companies like Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google are racing to build developer ecosystems. This community needs a home.

Demo-based hiring is here

Major tech companies and startups are increasingly asking candidates to submit demos instead of CVs. The hiring market is shifting — Antry is the infrastructure for this shift.

Vibe coding goes mainstream

AI-assisted coding tools have lowered the barrier to building drastically. A solo developer can now ship what used to require a team of five. This generation builds fast — and needs a platform built at their speed.

Community-led growth is proven

Platforms like Replit, Vercel, and Hugging Face have shown that developer communities built around tools and shared work grow virally. Antry applies the same model to the builder identity layer.
